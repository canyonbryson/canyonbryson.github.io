MiniPCR Project

DNA Extraction PCR Gel Electrophoresis

The objective: to simplify a MiniPCR kit, basically a lab experiment in a box, that can be rented by local schools to be performed by students.

Kit Description

Kit items include:

  1. MiniPCR: Performs PCR reactions safely
  2. BlueGel: Performs Gel Electrophoresis, comes with gel casting trays.
  3. GreenView Staining Dye: a safe alternative to Ethidium Bromide
  4. Reagents: The chemical mixes, enzymes, dna, or other parts needed for the reactions.
  5. Consumables: The materials that are consumed, or used, in the lab, like PCR tubes.
Experiment Description

The PTC experiment has the students experimentally confirm their ability to taste PTC, which genotype suggests if they find vegetables bitter. If you have the gene to taste PTC, you likely find vegetable bitter.

Experiment Steps:
  1. Students obtain a sample of their cells by scraping their inner cheek with a toothpick.
  2. These skin cells are then combined with some reagents to break down the cells and isolate the target DNA, then ran through an incubation period and PCR.
  3. The student then adds a restriction enzyme to their PCR product and put in an incubation period. The enzyme will then cut their DNA if they contain the gene to taste PTC.
  4. The DNA is then run through Gel Electrophoresis, where the student will visually see the length of their DNA. If they can taste PTC, their DNA strand will be shorter, as it was cut in half.

Molecular Biology

So what's the science behind this anyways? Let me explain. We'llseperate this experiment into 4 different steps:

  1. DNA Extraction/Isolation
  2. DNA duplication - PCR Reaction
  3. Gene Identification with Restriction Enzyme
  4. Gel Electrophoresis and Results Interpretation
First, DNA Extraction

After adding a sample of your inner cheek cells to the DNA Extraction Buffer, you let it incubate for 10 minutes. Here, the extraction buffer is breaking down your cell walls, releasing the DNA inside.

Second, DNA Duplication

PCR stands for Polymerase Chain Reaction, and it accomplished the task of duplicating your DNA. Inside your cells, and enzyme called DNA Polymerase polymerizes, or duplicates, your DNA with the help of a Helicase, which unzips your DNA. However, PCR works different. Instead of using Helicase to unzip your DNA, we heat it up to the point where it denatures - or separates from heat (This happens to proteins, too - this is why egg whites turn opaque when heated). At this high temperature, though, normal DNA Polymerase doesn't work. So we take some taq Polymerase, found in the hot beds in Yellostone, to do the job. We also guide the taq polymerase with a pair of primers - enzymes that attach to a specific portion of DNA, one for each strand of DNA, telling where the taq polymerase to start replicating. We create these primers ourselves. Once the DNA is duplicated, we can run through the cycle again, doubling the amount of DNA copies. After 30 cycles, you have created over 1 billion copies of that specific segment of DNA, each about 250 BP (base pairs, like AT or CG) long!

Third, Gene Identification

After PCR, you will incubate your DNA with a restriction enzyme for 15 minutes. This enzyme will cut your DNA you have a specific gene. This enzyme will cut your DNA if you carry the gene to taste the bitterness of vegetables (Note: this means your DNA carries the code for a protein that performes this function). Then each segment of DNA will be 100-150 bp long. If you dont have the gene, your dna segments stay at 250 bp long.

Fourth, Size determination and interpretation

To determine if your DNA is 250 bp or ~125 bp long, we run it through Gel Electrophoris. This uses electricity to pull your DNA through a gel. The smaller your DNA, the father it will travel. It will travel alongside a dna ladder, a mixture of dna segments of pre-determined length. We then compare your dna with the ladder, and interpret the results. The fastest way to see if you have the gene is if you have one band of DNA show or two - if you have two, your dna was cut into two pieces, each ~100 or ~150 bp long. You can then scientifically prove to your parents your a picky eater, and there's nothing you can do about it!
