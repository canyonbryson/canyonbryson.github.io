MiniPCR Project

DNA Extraction PCR Gel Electrophoresis

The objective: to simplify a MiniPCR kit, basically a lab experiment in a box, that can be rented by local schools to be performed by students.

Kit Description

Kit items include:

  1. MiniPCR: Performs PCR reactions safely
  2. BlueGel: Performs Gel Electrophoresis, comes with gel casting trays.
  3. GreenView Staining Dye: a safe alternative to Ethidium Bromide
  4. Reagents: The chemical mixes, enzymes, dna, or other parts needed for the reactions.
  5. Consumables: The materials that are consumed, or used, in the lab, like PCR tubes.
Experiment Description

The PTC experiment has the students experimentally confirm their ability to taste PTC, which genotype suggests if they find vegetables bitter. If you have the gene to taste PTC, you likely find vegetable bitter.

Experiment Steps:
  1. Students obtain a sample of their cells by scraping their inner cheek with a toothpick.
  2. These skin cells are then combined with some reagents to break down the cells and isolate the target DNA, then ran through an incubation period and PCR.
  3. The student then adds a restriction enzyme to their PCR product and put in an incubation period. The enzyme will then cut their DNA if they contain the gene to taste PTC.
  4. The DNA is then run through Gel Electrophoresis, where the student will visually see the length of their DNA. If they can taste PTC, their DNA strand will be shorter, as it was cut in half.
